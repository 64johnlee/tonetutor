## The exact 3-line on-screen dialogue (one clean take)
**Scenario: Restaurant · HSK 2.** Uses the classic 水饺/睡觉 tone mix-up so the tone error is obvious and funny.

**Line 1 — Lin Wei (AI):**
> 你好!想吃点什么?
> *Nǐ hǎo! Xiǎng chī diǎn shénme?*
> "Hi! What would you like to eat?"

**Line 2 — You (deliberate tone slip — this triggers the feedback):**
> Intend to say **我要水饺** (*Wǒ yào shuǐjiǎo* — "I want dumplings")
> Instead say **我要睡觉** (*Wǒ yào shuìjiào* — "I want to **sleep**") ← tones 3-3 → 4-4
> → ToneTutor flags the tone error. **This is beat 5 — record the REAL on-screen feedback.**

**Line 3 — Lin Wei (recovers, in character):**
> 哈哈,你是说水饺吧?好的,马上来!
> *Hāhā, nǐ shì shuō shuǐjiǎo ba? Hǎo de, mǎshàng lái!*
> "Haha, you mean dumplings, right? Sure — coming right up!"

> Why this line: shuǐjiǎo (dumplings) vs shuìjiào (sleep) is *the* famous Mandarin tone joke — relatable, funny, and it makes "why tones matter" instantly clear.

## Recording notes
- **Do one dry run first** and capture whatever feedback the app actually shows. Use the real text on screen — authenticity > script. If the app phrases the tone note differently, that's fine; show the real thing.
- If the 睡觉/水饺 error doesn't surface cleanly, fall back to another clear tone pair (e.g. 买 mǎi "buy" vs 卖 mài "sell").
- Record at real speed, then **speed-ramp the setup** (beats 2–4) and **slow down beat 5**.
- Export 1080×1920, captions burned in, royalty-free upbeat track low under the VO.
