# ToneTutor — University Outreach Tracker

Goal: get Chinese-language instructors to share ToneTutor (free practice tool) with students.
**This is your highest-trust, highest-volume channel** — one professor sharing it = a whole class of real users.

## How to use this list (don't skip — this is what makes it work)
1. **Don't guess emails.** For each target, open the department's **"People / Faculty / Contact"** page and find the **Chinese Language Program Coordinator / Director of Chinese Language / Senior Lecturer in Chinese** (the *language* teacher, not literature professors). Copy their real, current email into the table.
2. **Personalize every email** — name + one specific detail (e.g., "your CHIN 101 students"). Generic blasts get flagged/ignored.
3. **Batch ≤ 10/day** from a real address (use your `64lamei@` or a tonetutor@ address). Sending 100 at once = spam folder + domain reputation damage.
4. **One follow-up** after 5–7 days if no reply, then stop.
5. **Include an opt-out line** ("If you'd rather not hear about this, just reply 'no thanks'") — keeps you compliant and respectful.
6. Track status in the table so you don't double-send.

## The email (tighter version — see LAUNCH.md for the long one)
> **Subject:** A free AI Mandarin speaking tool for your [CHIN 101 / Chinese] students
>
> Dear Professor [Name],
>
> I'm a native Mandarin speaker and voice actor. I built **ToneTutor** — a free web tool where students practice real Mandarin conversations with an AI native speaker that grades their **tones** and grammar in real time (pinyin + audio, no signup).
>
> It targets the speaking-practice gap between class and fluency. Free to try: https://tonetutor.tefusiang.com — I'd be glad to give your class free access.
>
> Would you be open to sharing it with your students? Happy to do a 10-min call.
>
> Thank you,
> [Your name]
> *(If you'd rather not hear about this, just reply and I won't follow up.)*

## Beyond universities (often higher-converting — do after Tier 1)
- **CLTA** (Chinese Language Teachers Association, USA) — members teach K-12 + college; their listserv/forum reaches thousands.
- **AP Chinese teachers** — College Board AP Chinese community / Facebook groups.
- **r/ChineseLanguage teacher threads**, language-school directors, community/heritage Mandarin schools.
- **Study-abroad / Confucius Classroom** coordinators (where still active).

## Finding each email fast
Google: `"[University]" Chinese language program coordinator email` or go to the dept page → **People** → filter for "Chinese" + "Lecturer/Language". Many depts also list a general program email (e.g. an `...@univ.edu` office address) — fine to use if no individual coordinator is listed.
